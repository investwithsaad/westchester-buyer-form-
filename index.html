What clients say

In Their Words

A more analytical way to buy in Westchester

Westchester isn't one market. It's dozens of them.

Neighboring towns can differ meaningfully in property taxes, commute options, housing stock, pricing, inventory, and how much competition you'll run into on a given listing. A listing site will give you square footage and photos — it won't tell you why two homes ten minutes apart are priced so differently, or how a specific street compares on the numbers that actually matter to you.

I can also help you understand district boundaries and point you to objective, third-party school information for the properties and towns you're considering — the kind of homework that's easy to skip and hard to redo after you've made an offer.

How the plan works

A simple, three-step process.

1. Clarify your priorities and readiness

Schools, commute, taxes, space, and financing — we get specific about what actually matters before touring anything.

2. Compare towns and homes using relevant data

Tax rates, commute times, and inventory for the specific towns and listings you're weighing against each other — not general impressions.

3. Evaluate opportunities and build an offer strategy

When a home is worth pursuing, I'll give you an honest read on value and help you put together offer terms that hold up through inspection and appraisal.
