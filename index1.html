Why work with a Westchester specialist

Westchester isn't one market. It's dozens of them.

Something I try to explain to every buyer early on: two towns ten minutes apart can have completely different school ratings, tax rates, and commute times to Grand Central. A listing site will give you square footage and photos. It won't tell you why a home in Rye costs more than a similar one in Ossining, whether that "great school district" line actually holds up for the specific street you're looking at, or how many offers a house like this one has been getting lately.

That's usually where I end up being useful — especially for people relocating from the city who haven't had years to absorb all of this the slow way.

How the plan works

How I actually work with buyers.

1. We figure out what actually matters to you

Schools, commute, walkability, taxes, lot size — these usually matter more than the listing photos. I'd rather spend an hour on this upfront than waste a Saturday touring homes that were never going to work.

2. We compare towns with real numbers

School data, effective tax rates, actual Metro-North commute times on the Harlem, Hudson, and New Haven lines, and how competitive a town has been lately — not just what people say about a place.

3. When it's right, we move — and I'll tell you if it's not

Westchester's best towns are often competitive, so when the right home shows up, I'll give you an honest read on it and help you put together an offer that actually holds up through inspection and appraisal. And if something feels off about a property, I'll say so, even if that's not what you want to hear.
